Reaching the next billion users

Who are the next billion users?

Yuki: A Yoga Teacher who has AD(H)D (1/2)

Yuki could not really explain her apparent forgetfulness and not being able to focus or complete tasks. She knew that if she came across a long document or web page with dense text she had to find the key points. If the web page failed to have a clear structure, well-spaced and highlighted headings she would be lost and lose concentration. Yuki also said that if she was reading her mobile screen, advertisements appearing between chunks of text upset her focus and she had to stop reading.

Yuki: A Yoga Teacher who has AD(H) (2/2)

However, when there was good use of white space, recognizable icons linking to simple bold text clarifying the important points, Yuki could target these areas and find out what she needed.

A clear summary helped Yuki understand and she could remember much of what she had read.

Goal

Reach the next billion users, by…

Developing support for personalization of content on the web.

Mechanisms to achieve this

Providing the means for authors to supply element-level semantics.

Authoring/Bootstrapping

  • The approach needs input from authors.
  • We aim to make this as easy as possible.
  • We've seen things like <main> work very well, because there is wide potential applicability/neatness for developers.
  • If we can show the potential gains for users, we can create demand…
  • …and the sorts of things we’re expressing are quite fundamental.

W3C Personalization Task Force

  • Part of the Accessible Platform Architectures Working Group
  • …within the Web Accessibility Initiative
  • …of the World Wide Web Consortium.
  • More importantly: part of your web!

Began with ARIA and WCAG 1.0

  • Personalization was the aim from the start
  • Focus at first on scripts and screen readers: avoiding undue burden.
  • ARIA then split from COGA (under APA and AG)
  • Codified ‘input purpose’ in WCAG 2.1 (SC 1.3.5)
  • Personalization was created to carry on this work
  • Informational, suggestive attributes available to authors that do not override other semantics.
  • Now ready for candidate review status

This is BIG

  • Multiple stages to the plan
    • Personalization Semantics Explainer
    • Module 1 Adaptable Content (nearing wide community review)
    • Module 2 Adaptable Help and Support
    • Module 3 Adaptable Tools
  • Years in the making, and to come—get in on the ground floor!

This is REALLY BIG

  • Focus on cognition, cognitive disabilities and usability.
    • Related: Making Content Usable for People with Cognitive and Learning Disabilities
  • Universally applicable (to varying degrees).
  • Could help make the web a lot more mailable and adaptive for people.
  • Built on simple and robust tech, so has a good chance!
  • …with much room to grow later on.

Personalization Semantics Module 1

  • Programmatically identify the purpose of various interface elements.
  • Support simplification of user interfaces and content.
    • Showing what’s important.
    • Removing distractions.
  • Support presenting symbols instead of words.
  • …and more!
